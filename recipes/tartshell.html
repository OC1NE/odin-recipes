Tart Shells

Pâte Sablée

Pâte sablée is a classic French shortcrust pastry dough that's used to make tart shells and other desserts. It is known for its delicate, shortbread crumble and sandy texture. It is rich in flavor due to the high butter content.

I found this recipe on youtube by: Hanbit Cho. He is a pastry chef based in Seoul, Korea. Go checkout his page and discover many great recipes.

The recipe below is taken straight from Hanbits Tart Shell Masterclass video on youtube which I will embed below

Ingredients

  • Unsalted Butter (room temp) 100g
  • Salt a pinch
  • Powdered Sugar 72g
  • Almond Powder 20g
  • Corn Starch 30g
  • Eggs (room temp)36g
  • Cake Flour 170g

Directions

  1. Beat the room temp butter lightly (add in salt).
  2. Sift in powdered sugar, almond powder and corn starch. Beat lightly.
  3. Add the room temp. eggs gradually and mix.
  4. Finally, add in the cake flour and mix well.
  5. Roll it out to 2mm/3mm thickness between parchment papers. (2mm thickness for 6~8cm diameter, 3mm thickness for 14~18cm thickness. Any other sizes, you decide)
  6. Rest in the fridge for 1~2hrs.
  7. For regular tart pan: Cut it out into one big disc. For perforated tart ring: Cut it out into strips/discs
  8. For regular tart pan: Line the tart pan. Prick the bottom. Place a sheet of parchment paper and then pie weights.
  9. For perforated tart ring: Line the ring. Place it on a perforated mat.
  10. For regular tart pan: Bake at 170℃ for 10mins, take the pie weights out, then bake for another 5 mins. (160℃ for 2mm)
  11. For perforated tart ring: Bake at 170℃ for 15mins. (160℃ for 2mm)
